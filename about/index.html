Hello! I’m Tamara. I create custom nails for amazing women of all sorts.

I’ve always considered myself to be the one who wanders off from the common road to find my own path. I was never at peace for working regular 9-5 jobs because the status quo doesn’t provide enough intellectual and creative fulfillment for me.

Today, I help maintain growing, healthy, precise, and gorgeous nails that can compliment my clients’ style and contribute to their confidence. With my business, I hope to inspire other women to pursue what they love and enjoy the journey every step of the way. Thanks for spending your time learning more about me. Read more—to learn more!

Through the years of high school and college, I honed in on what I did naturally, create. So as a senior in college, I searched for and attended a manicuring school full-time for three months (Let’s not forget commuting an hour to get there). In 2015, I obtained my BA degree in May and my Manicurist License two months later in July.

With the goal of mastering my craft before I performed on the public, I took the first year to gather experience. So in the summer of 2016, I began my journey as a public Nail Tech. Ever since, the experience of a self-employed, entrepreneur hasn’t been perfect, but very much so worth it.

MORE ABOUT ME

My story and how I ended up here

pupose and mindset

My mission statement as being a Black Nail Tech is to be black excellence. From marketing to customer service, I want to give my clients an experience of refreshing professionalism along with amazing nails. Being genuine to each and every client down to the products I use, consultation, being reliable, and relatable is the gratifying reason why I love doing nails.

background

Starting at the age of 14, I dabbled with doing my own nails along with other hobbies that involved creativity. Nails was a special outlet for me and I began to pursue it seriously while in college as an art student. With my art foundation, it was easier for me to apply my knowledge of sculpture and detailed painting to nails. While in manicuring school, I got the opportunity to work with Crystal at Nail Yeah! and be surrounded by classmates who loved doing nails also. I graduated from manicuring school a month before graduating college with my degree.

After graduating, I began investing into my skill; starting from the bottom. It took me an entire year to get to the point where wanted to be. I wanted to be sure I wasn’t practicing on the public. After moving back to my hometown (Winston-Salem) in 2016, I began my journey as a black nail tech.
